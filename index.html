The Detailed Guide To Spotting At East Midlands Airport

This website is designed with the Spotters of East Midlands Airport in mind. With regular updates on interesting movements, schedules, spotting locations, featured photos from in and around the airport and a whole lot more!

What makes East Midlands Airport so special?

Visiting East Midlands Airport in the mid-day hours may lead you to think of it as just another regional airport with your usual Ryanair, Jet2 and TUI, however anyone who knows the airport well will tell you otherwise. At night, the airport transforms into a cargo megahub processing over 1,000 tonnes of cargo every day. Even during times when aviation is seemingly put to a halt, EMA keeps on moving forward - during the Coronavirus outbreak, East Midlands saw the smallest drop in flight numbers of any European airport. East Midlands is the UK's busiest pure cargo airport with airlines such as DHL, UPS and West Atlantic utilising EMA a hub. EMA also receives many interesting movements due to it being the airport of choice for Formula 1 teams to ship cars and equipment to and from races around the world. Furthermore, the Antanov 124 makes a visit to the airport every now and then alongside the IL-76 - even the famous Antanov 225 has visited before.

What aircraft will I see at East Midlands?

Current aircraft that you will see on most days of the week include the ATP, 737, 757, 767, 777, A300, and A330. You may also see 747s and other rarer aircraft every now and then.
